# Kelza Investor Experience

> "People don't invest in slides.
>
> People invest in understanding.
>
> This experience exists to create understanding."

---

# Purpose

Kelza should never present itself as a traditional startup pitch deck.

Investors have already seen thousands of websites.

Thousands of decks.

Thousands of timelines.

Thousands of market charts.

Most of them are forgotten within minutes.

Kelza should not compete by adding more information.

Kelza should compete by creating a memorable experience.

Every animation.

Every transition.

Every illustration.

Every product reveal.

Every interaction.

Every mockup.

Every sentence.

Should answer one investor question.

The investor should never feel they are reading.

They should feel they are discovering.

---

The experience is divided into four acts.

# ACT I

## FEEL

Duration

2–3 minutes

Goal

Create curiosity.

Not understanding.

At this stage we are not explaining Kelza.

We are making the investor curious enough to continue.

Questions inside the investor's mind

"What is this?"

"This doesn't look like a startup website."

"Where is this going?"

Desired emotion

Wonder.

Curiosity.

Anticipation.

Visual Language

Universe.

Particles.

Light.

Connections.

Movement.

Depth.

Minimal text.

No statistics.

No charts.

No product.

Only emotion.

# ACT II

## UNDERSTAND

Goal

Answer

"What problem are they solving?"

Do NOT attack existing companies.

Do NOT criticize social media.

Kelza wants to become better at helping people discover meaningful communities, creators and relationships.

Investor Questions

Why does this company exist?

Who is this for?

Why would anyone care?

Visuals

Real product.

Real people.

Real communities.

Real events.

Real creators.

Real app.

Less text.

More demonstration.
